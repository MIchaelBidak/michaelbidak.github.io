Say hi

Your inbox is full of recruiters who don’t know you.

Another InMail. Another “perfect fit” that isn’t. Another “Hi Firstname” from someone who read the top line of your profile and nothing else.

Here’s the thing: you’re probably not even unhappy. But every engineer has the quiet 3 p.m. question — is this still the right place to do my best work? And with this many recruiters in your inbox, answering it should be easy.

It isn’t.

Volume isn’t attention. What actually helps is one person who knows your work, knows the market, and has no reason to push you anywhere you don’t want to go. That’s the job. That’s the whole job.

A headhunter in your corner

Working with me costs you nothing — companies pay my fee, and the only outcome I get paid for is a move that sticks. Here’s what that buys you:

Confidential representation. Your search doesn’t exist until you say it does. Your résumé goes nowhere without your permission, your name never leaves the room, and if you decide to stay put, nobody’s the wiser.

Career-goal mapping. Before you hear about a single role, we get clear on the destination — the work, the team, the trajectory you actually want — so every introduction moves you forward instead of just sideways.

Comp and offer guidance. Real numbers on what the market pays for what you do, and a negotiator in your corner when the offer lands. You’ll never wonder whether you left money on the table.

Honest feedback, either way. If a role isn’t right for you, I’ll say so. If I think you’re underselling yourself, I’ll say that too. I’d rather earn your trust for next year than force a fit this quarter.

Meet Mike Bidak, the human behind the search

I studied psychology before I ever read a résumé, and it shows. After years recruiting engineers both in‑house and agency‑side, the pattern never changed: the moves that stick start with a person, not a keyword search. So that’s how I work — I learn what you’re great at, what you’re done with, and what you’d actually move for. Then I go find it.

One honest note: I’m not an engineer, and I won’t pretend to be one. You’ll never get a surprise whiteboard quiz from me. What you’ll get is someone who has spent his career around great engineers, knows what companies are really paying, and picks up the phone when you call.

How it works

We start with fifteen minutes, human to human. No screening script, no “walk me through your résumé.” Just where you are, what’s working, what isn’t, and what would actually make you move. If the answer is “nothing right now,” that’s a perfectly good answer.

You won’t hear from me weekly with “exciting opportunities.” When I bring you a role, it’s because it matches what you told me mattered — the work, the team, the comp, the trajectory. You can pass in one word and I’ll keep looking. Your name goes nowhere without a yes from you first.

When the right one lands, I stay in it with you: comp data for the negotiation, a sanity check on the counteroffer your boss will make, and help with the resignation conversation everyone dreads. Through the signature — and I’ll still be checking in after your first week.
